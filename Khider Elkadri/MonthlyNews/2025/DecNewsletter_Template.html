Market Update: Why Declining Rates and Home Values Can Work in Your Favor

The housing and mortgage markets are shifting once again — and this time, the changes may bring real opportunities. Recently, we’ve seen a decline in both mortgage rates and home values across many markets, and Calgary is no exception. While that may sound like a sign of uncertainty, it can actually open the door to significant advantages for both buyers and homeowners.

What Lower Rates Mean for You

Mortgage rates have softened in the market — for example, some 5-year fixed insured rates are now in the mid to high 3% range.

Lower intrest rates can:

  • ►Increase your buying power:
    With a lower rate, you can qualify for a higher-priced home while keeping your monthly payment the same.
  • ►Lower your payments:
    If you already own a home, refinancing into a lower rate can reduce your monthly costs and save you thousands over time. Especially when adding your high interest debts into your new mortgage rates.
  • ►Free up cash flow:
    Lower payments mean more flexibility for savings, investments, or home improvements.

How Lower Home Values Create Opportunity

While it can be unsettling to see home prices soften, in Calgary the shift is producing buyer-friendly conditions:

  • ►The benchmark residential price across Calgary in June 2025 was reported at $586,200, which is down 3.6% year-over-year. Real-estate-homes.ca
  • ►In September 2025 the benchmark price was $572,800, a 4.0% decline year-over-year and down about 0.7% from the prior month. Inventory climbed ~36% year-over-year, and the months of supply rose to about 4.0 months — signalling a shift toward more balanced (buyer-favourable) conditions. WOWA
  • ►For higher-density product types (apartments/row homes), the price declines have been more pronounced, while detached homes have held up better. Real-estate-homes.ca

So what does that mean for you?

  • ►Less competition: Fewer to no bidding wars and more negotiating power for buyers.
  • ►Better deals: Accessible opportunities in neighbourhoods that may have been unattainable during the peak.
  • ►Strategic timing: If you buy when values are softer and rates are low, you’re well positioned when the market rebounds.

The Big Picture

Periods of declining rates and values don’t last forever. These moments often represent a window of opportunity—for buyers to get into the market affordably, and for current homeowners to strengthen their financial position through smart refinancing.
